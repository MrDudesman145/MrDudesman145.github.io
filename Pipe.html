Pipe Welding

Pipe welding is a specialized form of welding used to join pipes together, crucial in industries like oil and gas, chemical processing, and utilities. It employs various welding techniques, primarily arc welding processes like MIG and TIG welding, to create secure and leak-proof connections. The integrity of pipe welds is vital for the safe transport of materials, making it a critical skill in these industries.

What You Learn in a Class

A pipe welding class provides training in the specialized techniques required to join pipes using various welding processes. These classes typically cover both the theoretical and practical aspects of pipe welding, including blueprint reading, safety procedures, and hands-on experience with different welding methods. Students will learn to make satisfactory welds on ferrous pipe, understand proper joint fit-up, and potentially prepare for industry-recognized certifications.
